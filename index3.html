Scenario-Based Testing

We provide following testing scenarios to secure your organization privacy and assets:

  • Identify potential and hidden exploits
  • Validate security posture to prevent, detect and respond to cyber threats
  • Prepare to distinguish between true and false positive alerts
  • Analyze Incident response plan effectiveness
  • Business related common threats testing and validation
  • Gather results from any lesson learned during previous incidents

Breach and attack simulations

This engagement includes the following main services:

  • Conduct for advanced security testing
  • Identify vulnerabilities in security environments by simulating possible attacks
  • Breach and attack simulations conducted like a continuous, automated penetration test
  • Prepare the business to handle a real attack with required action plan
  • Simulation can be internal or external attack to cover in scope targets
  • Policies and procedures improvement

Cloud Security Assessment

We perform evaluation, tests, and analysis of your cloud infrastructure to ensure that your organization is protected from a variety of security risks and threats. We also detect weaknesses and potential points of entry within cloud infrastructure, analyze network for any possible exploitation to prevent future attacks. Cloud assessment offers following benefits.

  • Reduced risk from accidental misconfiguration
  • Improved resilience
  • Optimize security posture
  • Efficient account management
  • Enhanced possible compromise detection
  • Maintain regulatory compliance and certifications

Red Teaming

Our red team exercise is a customized advance security assessments tailored to your organization's security requirements. It simulates possible targeted attack types and methods used by malicious actors across different threat levels and provides evidence based results. This engagement includes following key objectives.

  • Prepare for real world cyber attacks
  • Test the strength and effectiveness of current security posture
  • Enhance incident response plan
  • Improve risk mitigation techniques
  • Provide guidance for security roadmap and future investment
  • Uncover how a hacker can access sensitive information
  • Identify methods used to disrupt services, operations, and business

Frequently Asked Questions

Based on market competition and current technology adoption, networks are becoming more vulnerable and exploitable. At the same time, cyber attacks and data compromise incidents are becoming a real threat for most organizations. Vulnerability Assessment and Penetration testing helps an organization to validate the security posture against latest threats and identifies security risks and threats within current environment. Additionally, it also helps meet a wide range of compliance standards and regulatory requirements.

VA and PT differ from each other in two main aspects. The VA process provides a horizontal map into the security position of the target networks and applications, while the PT process vertically deep dives into the findings. In other words, the VA process shows how big a vulnerability is, while the PT validates the extensiveness of given finding. Also, due to the nature of work involved in each process, a VA can be carried out using automated tools, while a PT can be automated or a combination of automated and manual exploit testing. PT is also a simulation of what real hackers would do to your networks and applications.

VAPT should be conducted on a regular basis based on internal change cycles and regulatory compliance requirements. Most of the compliance standards require yearly penetration testing and quarterly internal and external vulnerability assessments. We recommend a quarterly VAPT process to meet current security challenges and compliance requirements.

We offer two free of cost retests if findings and gaps are detected during the initial VAPT engagement.

Yes, our VAPT reports are acceptable by all major compliance standards including but not limited to PCI, HIPAA, DFARS, NERC-CIP, CJIS and ISO 27001
